# Core Experience Loops

## 1. Vision
We do not design "game mechanics" in isolation; we design continuous loops of human experience. The Core Experience Loops are the minute-to-minute cycles that keep a player engaged in a state of flow, curiosity, and comfort.

## 2. Behavioral Theory
A loop consists of an Action, Feedback, a Reward (functional or emotional), and a Hook (a reason to take the next action). If any piece is missing, the loop breaks and the session ends prematurely.

## 3. Psychological Foundation
- **Flow State (Csikszentmihalyi):** The balance between challenge and skill. Since cozy/simulation games lack high-skill challenge, we replace "challenge" with "curiosity" or "creative expression" to maintain Flow.
- **Variable Ratio Reinforcement:** Providing rewards at unpredictable intervals is highly engaging (though we restrict this to discovery, not monetization).

## 4. Design Principles
- **The Curiosity Loop:** Question → Interaction → Discovery → New Question. (e.g., "What's under that rock?" → Lifts rock → Finds a key → "What does this key open?").
- **The Comfort Loop:** Routine → Familiarity → Relaxation → Return. (e.g., Logging in → Harvesting crops → Greeting neighbors → Logging out).
- **The Progress Loop:** Action → Visible Growth → Reward → New Goal.

## 5. Implementation Guidelines
Every core mechanic (e.g., planting a seed, clearing debris) must feed into one of these loops. The feedback must be immediate (within 100ms) and satisfying (visual/audio delight).

## 6. Player Journey
- Players enter the game seeking the Comfort Loop.
- While executing the Comfort Loop, they are presented with triggers for the Curiosity Loop.
- Completing Curiosity Loops feeds into the Progress Loop.

## 7. Behavioral KPIs
- Loop completion rate (does the player finish the cycle once started?).
- Loops per session (measure of session depth).

## 8. Success Metrics
- Average session length of 15-25 minutes (healthy, not exhausting).
- D1 Retention > 50%.

## 9. Common Mistakes
- **Dead Ends:** Giving a reward that doesn't trigger a new question or goal.
- **Grind:** Extending the Action phase of the loop without providing intermediate Feedback.

## 10. Ethical Considerations
Loops must not be infinite scrolling mechanisms designed to trap players. There must be natural, satisfying stopping points where a player feels good about putting the game down.

## 11. Future Expansion
Mapping sensory feedback combinations (VFX + SFX) to specific stages of the loops to create a unified subconscious language for the player.
