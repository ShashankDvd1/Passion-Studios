# Human Experience (HX) Principles

## 1. Vision
We do not build games to extract time and money; we build interactive worlds that enrich players' lives. The Human Experience (HX) Engine ensures every system we build prioritizes the emotional well-being, meaningful engagement, and long-term satisfaction of the player.

## 2. Behavioral Theory
Players do not return for mechanics; they return for the emotions those mechanics evoke. By shifting our focus from behavioral conditioning (which can be coercive) to emotional resonance, we foster intrinsic motivation rather than extrinsic dependency.

## 3. Psychological Foundation
Built upon Self-Determination Theory (Deci & Ryan), prioritizing three psychological needs:
1. **Autonomy:** Players must feel in control of their choices, never coerced.
2. **Competence:** Players must experience meaningful growth and mastery.
3. **Relatedness:** Players must feel a sense of belonging and connection, whether to characters, the world, or other players.

## 4. Design Principles
- **Emotion First:** Design the feeling, then design the mechanic to support it.
- **Respect Player Time:** Session lengths should be satisfying, not infinite. It should be easy to put the game down.
- **Intrinsic Over Extrinsic:** Rewards should amplify the joy of the activity, not replace it as the sole motivation.
- **Sustainable Engagement:** Optimize for 3 years of play, not 3 weeks of addiction.

## 5. Implementation Guidelines
When designing a feature, agents must map the feature to a target emotion (see `04-003: Emotion_Framework`). If the feature relies on FOMO, frustration, or negative reinforcement to drive engagement, it must be redesigned.

## 6. Player Journey
- **Day 1:** Discovery and Wonder.
- **Day 7:** Comfort and Routine.
- **Day 30:** Ownership and Identity.
- **Day 365+:** Mastery and Community.

## 7. Behavioral KPIs
- Ratio of positive emotions to negative emotions (measured via sentiment analysis).
- Session length variance (consistent session lengths indicate healthy integration into daily life).
- Return rate after prolonged absences (measures lasting attachment).

## 8. Success Metrics
- Happy Retained Players (HRP).
- Long-term (D90+) retention rates.
- Qualitative player sentiment scores (App Store reviews, community feedback).

## 9. Common Mistakes
- **Confusing compulsion with enjoyment:** High engagement metrics driven by FOMO are toxic.
- **Over-rewarding:** Extinguishing intrinsic motivation by attaching a currency reward to every action.
- **Designing for whales:** Ruining the core loop for the majority to extract value from a minority.

## 10. Ethical Considerations
We absolutely prohibit:
- Paywalls that halt progress.
- Manipulative dark patterns (e.g., hidden costs, fake urgency).
- Loot boxes or gambling mechanics.
- Optimizing for frustration (creating a problem just to sell the solution).

## 11. Future Expansion
As our understanding of player psychology evolves, this document will expand to include more nuanced frameworks for different player psychographics (e.g., Bartle's Taxonomy adapted for cozy simulation).
