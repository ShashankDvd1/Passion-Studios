# Player Psychology

## 1. Vision
To design an experience that players love, we must deeply understand how players think, process information, and form mental models. This document outlines the cognitive and psychological principles that guide our UX and game design.

## 2. Behavioral Theory
Players operate using two cognitive systems (Kahneman's System 1 and System 2). The core loop must live in System 1 (intuitive, fluid, low-effort), while meta-loops and strategy engage System 2 (analytical, deliberate).

## 3. Psychological Foundation
- **Cognitive Load Theory:** Working memory is limited. UI and mechanics must not overwhelm it.
- **Gestalt Principles:** Players perceive patterns and structures, not isolated elements.
- **Loss Aversion:** Players hate losing things more than they enjoy gaining them. (Use carefully, and never coercively).
- **Endowed Progress Effect:** Players are more motivated to complete a goal if they feel they have already made artificial progress toward it.

## 4. Design Principles
- **Minimize Friction:** The path to the core fun should have zero cognitive friction.
- **Chunking Information:** Introduce complex systems one piece at a time (progressive disclosure).
- **Clarity Over Cleverness:** UX should be immediately understandable; do not reinvent standard conventions unnecessarily.
- **Forgiveness:** Players should be able to recover from mistakes easily without severe penalties.

## 5. Implementation Guidelines
- Tutorialize through action, not text.
- Use visual hierarchy to guide the eye to the most important action.
- When introducing a new mechanic, isolate it from other variables so the player can form a correct mental model.

## 6. Player Journey
- **Onboarding:** High hand-holding, extremely low cognitive load. Goal: immediate success.
- **Early Game:** Gradual introduction of systems. Goal: building competence.
- **Mid Game:** Combining systems. Goal: flow state.
- **End Game:** Mastery and optimization. Goal: self-expression.

## 7. Behavioral KPIs
- Tutorial completion rate (measures onboarding cognitive load).
- Time to first core loop completion.
- Error rates on core UI actions.

## 8. Success Metrics
- 95%+ Tutorial completion.
- Player feedback indicating the game is "easy to learn" but "deep."

## 9. Common Mistakes
- **Information Dump:** Explaining everything upfront.
- **Punishing Experimentation:** Making it costly to try new strategies, leading to anxiety.
- **Inconsistent Mental Models:** A mechanic working one way here, and a different way there.

## 10. Ethical Considerations
We must not exploit cognitive biases (like the sunk cost fallacy) to manipulate players into spending money. We respect the player's cognitive energy and do not exhaust it deliberately.

## 11. Future Expansion
Deepening the cognitive load models specifically for different player archetypes (e.g., neurodivergent players, casual vs. midcore players).
