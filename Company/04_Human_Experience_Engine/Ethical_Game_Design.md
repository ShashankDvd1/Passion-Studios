# Ethical Game Design

## 1. Vision
We do not optimize for engagement at all costs; we optimize for *healthy* engagement. This document defines the absolute, non-negotiable boundaries of our game design, ensuring we never exploit our players.

## 2. Behavioral Theory
Short-term monetization often relies on cognitive biases and psychological vulnerabilities (e.g., sunk cost fallacy, fear of missing out). While effective in the short term, they destroy long-term trust and brand equity.

## 3. Psychological Foundation
- **Autonomy Support:** Players must feel their choices are their own, free from manipulative pressure.
- **Cognitive Transparency:** Players should accurately understand the odds, costs, and time required for any in-game action.

## 4. Design Principles (The Hard Boundaries)
1. **No Pay-to-Win:** Spending money cannot provide a competitive advantage.
2. **No Paywalls:** All core gameplay content must be accessible without spending money.
3. **No Loot Boxes / Gacha:** We do not sell randomized chances for items using real-world currency (directly or via premium currency).
4. **No Artificial Pain:** We do not create intentionally frustrating mechanics (e.g., extremely limited inventory, agonizingly slow walk speeds) purely to sell the solution.
5. **No Toxic FOMO:** Limited-time events must be achievable with a normal play schedule.

## 5. Implementation Guidelines
- Monetization must focus on Expression (cosmetics, decorations) and Convenience (small time-savers that do not replace gameplay).
- Every monetization feature must pass a review by the Founder Agent to ensure compliance with this framework.

## 6. Player Journey
The player should feel respected from Day 1 to Day 365. They should spend money because they *want* to support the game and express themselves, not because they feel they *have* to in order to progress.

## 7. Behavioral KPIs
- Ratio of paying to non-paying users (healthy conversion).
- Support tickets related to buyer's remorse or feeling cheated.

## 8. Success Metrics
- Zero regulatory complaints or violations.
- A reputation as a "player-first, ethical studio" within the gaming community.

## 9. Common Mistakes
- **The Boiling Frog:** Slowly introducing slightly manipulative mechanics over time until the game violates its own ethical principles.
- **Obfuscated Currencies:** Forcing players to buy "Gems" to buy "Tokens" to buy an item, intentionally confusing the real-dollar cost.

## 10. Ethical Considerations
This entire document is the ethical consideration. It is the highest law of the Human Experience Engine.

## 11. Future Expansion
Adapting these rules as new technologies (e.g., Web3, Generative AI) introduce new potential ethical gray areas.
