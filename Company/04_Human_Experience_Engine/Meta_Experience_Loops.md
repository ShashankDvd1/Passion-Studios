# Meta Experience Loops

## 1. Vision
If Core Loops are why a player plays for 20 minutes, Meta Loops are why a player returns for 20 weeks. Meta loops provide the overarching context, purpose, and long-term narrative to the minute-to-minute actions.

## 2. Behavioral Theory
Long-term engagement requires goals that transcend a single session. Meta loops bridge sessions by creating investments that mature over time.

## 3. Psychological Foundation
- **Zeigarnik Effect:** People remember uncompleted or interrupted tasks better than completed ones. A session should end with an uncompleted long-term task.
- **Endowment Effect:** People value things more highly once they own them (or build them).

## 4. Design Principles
- **The Ownership Loop:** Investment → Customization → Attachment → Protection → Return.
- **The Collection Loop:** Discovery → Collection → Completion → Prestige → Exploration.
- **The Narrative Loop:** Mystery → Clues → Revelation → New Mystery.

## 5. Implementation Guidelines
Meta loops should progress much slower than core loops. Earning a currency (Core) happens constantly; spending that currency to upgrade a house (Meta) happens occasionally. The Meta loop provides the *reason* for the Core loop.

## 6. Player Journey
- **Days 1-3:** Driven primarily by Core Loops. Meta loops are introduced but barely progressed.
- **Days 7-14:** Meta loops take over as the primary motivation for logging in.
- **Days 30+:** The player's identity and attachment (Ownership Loop) sustain engagement.

## 7. Behavioral KPIs
- Meta-goal setting behavior (do players manually track or set goals?).
- Progression velocity (is the meta pacing too fast/slow?).

## 8. Success Metrics
- D30 and D90 Retention.
- Conversion rate from D7 to D30.

## 9. Common Mistakes
- **Disconnected Meta:** The Core loop actions don't contribute meaningfully to the Meta loop (e.g., a puzzle game where the meta-city building feels bolted on).
- **Goal Exhaustion:** Reaching the end of a Meta loop without a new one opening up, causing churn.

## 10. Ethical Considerations
Do not use "appointment mechanics" (e.g., your crops will wither and die if you don't log in every 4 hours) as a Meta loop. Punitive meta loops violate our ethical framework. We use positive anticipation, not negative reinforcement.

## 11. Future Expansion
Developing a "Community Meta Loop" for asynchronous multiplayer interactions.
