# Ownership System

## 1. Vision
Players do not abandon worlds they have helped build. The Ownership System designs for deep personal investment, transitioning the player's mindset from "I am playing a game" to "This is my space."

## 2. Behavioral Theory
Investment of time, effort, and creativity leads to attachment. When players customize, arrange, and nurture elements in a game, they form an emotional bond with those elements.

## 3. Psychological Foundation
- **IKEA Effect:** People place a disproportionately high value on things they partially created or assembled themselves.
- **Endowment Effect:** People value an object more highly simply because they own it.
- **Identity Projection:** Avatars and player-spaces serve as digital extensions of the self.

## 4. Design Principles
- **Meaningful Customization:** Give players choices that allow for true aesthetic expression, not just functional upgrades.
- **Nurturing Mechanics:** Require the player to care for something (a garden, a pet, a village) over time.
- **Permanent Impact:** The player's actions must leave permanent, visible marks on the game world.
- **Display and Share:** Allow players to proudly display their owned items or spaces to others.

## 5. Implementation Guidelines
- **Friction in Creation:** Do not make customization instant. It should take time to gather materials and build furniture (triggering the IKEA effect).
- **Naming:** Always allow players to name things (pets, towns, tools). Naming is the fastest shortcut to emotional ownership.
- **Scarcity:** Some customization items must be rare or difficult to obtain to have value.

## 6. Player Journey
- **Day 1:** Given a basic space. Minimal attachment.
- **Day 7:** First major customization choice made. Sense of agency.
- **Day 30:** The space reflects the player's personality. High attachment.
- **Day 90+:** The space is a digital home. Deep emotional investment.

## 7. Behavioral KPIs
- Customization interaction rate (time spent in decoration/customization menus).
- Utilization of naming features.
- Screenshot/sharing rates of player spaces.

## 8. Success Metrics
- High D30 and D90 retention among players who engage with customization.
- Community emergence around sharing designs.

## 9. Common Mistakes
- **Illusion of Choice:** Offering three customization options that all look basically the same.
- **Forced Resets:** Wiping player progress or forcing them to abandon their built spaces without massive emotional payoff.
- **Paywalling Expression:** Locking all meaningful aesthetic expression behind premium currency.

## 10. Ethical Considerations
We must not use the Endowment Effect to manipulate players into spending money to "protect" their investments from arbitrary destruction.

## 11. Future Expansion
Integration with social systems to allow "house tours" and community-voted design contests.
