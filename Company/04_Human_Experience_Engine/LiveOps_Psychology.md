# LiveOps Psychology

## 1. Vision
LiveOps (Live Operations) shouldn't feel like a store update; it should feel like the world is alive. Seasonal changes, festivals, and dynamic events create a shared sense of time and community urgency without relying on toxic FOMO.

## 2. Behavioral Theory
Human psychology is deeply tied to seasons, rhythms, and collective experiences. Breaking the static nature of software with time-bound, thematic events creates spikes of massive engagement.

## 3. Psychological Foundation
- **Anticipation (Dopaminergic System):** Anticipating an event often provides more dopamine than the event itself.
- **Scarcity Principle:** Things available for a limited time are perceived as more valuable.
- **Social Proof:** Seeing everyone else participate in an event makes it highly compelling to join.

## 4. Design Principles
- **The Tease:** Announce events early in the game world (e.g., NPCs talking about an upcoming festival, a mysterious tent being built).
- **Theming:** Events must fundamentally change the aesthetic of the world (snow, autumn leaves, nighttime festivals) to break visual fatigue.
- **Forgiving FOMO:** Limited-time items must return eventually (e.g., next year). Absolute, permanent exclusion creates resentment, not healthy urgency.

## 5. Implementation Guidelines
- Run one major event per month (1-2 weeks duration) and one minor event per week (weekend duration).
- Ensure core loops integrate with the event (e.g., harvesting crops during the Summer Festival yields "Festival Tokens").

## 6. Player Journey
- **Tease Phase:** Curiosity, Anticipation.
- **Launch Phase:** Excitement, High Engagement.
- **Grind Phase:** Routine, Goal-oriented behavior.
- **Conclusion Phase:** Satisfaction, Closure.

## 7. Behavioral KPIs
- Event participation rate (percentage of active DAU engaging in event mechanics).
- Lift in DAU during the first 3 days of an event.

## 8. Success Metrics
- Sustained revenue and engagement spikes during events without a massive post-event churn crash.
- Positive community sentiment regarding event rewards.

## 9. Common Mistakes
- **Event Fatigue:** Running events back-to-back with zero downtime, exhausting the player base.
- **Pay-to-Complete:** Making event goals mathematically impossible to achieve without spending real money.

## 10. Ethical Considerations
We use the Scarcity Principle to make the world feel dynamic, not to extort players. Event rewards must be achievable through reasonable play schedules.

## 11. Future Expansion
Dynamic LiveOps triggered by community behavior (e.g., a "Rainy Season" triggers if the community harvests 1 million water crops).
