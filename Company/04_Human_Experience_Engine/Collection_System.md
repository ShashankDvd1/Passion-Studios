# Collection System

## 1. Vision
Collecting is a fundamental human drive. The Collection System provides a long-term, low-pressure meta-loop that rewards exploration, persistence, and dedication without enforcing stressful deadlines.

## 2. Behavioral Theory
Collections provide structure to unstructured play. When a player isn't sure what to do next, the collection log provides an immediate, clear goal.

## 3. Psychological Foundation
- **The Zeigarnik Effect:** A partially filled collection book creates cognitive tension that drives the player to complete it.
- **Foraging Instinct:** Evolutionary psychology suggests humans derive immense satisfaction from finding and gathering rare resources.

## 4. Design Principles
- **Tangible Display:** Collections must be viewable in the game world (a museum, a scrapbook, a shelf), not just as a UI checklist.
- **Tiered Rarity:** A mix of common items (constant reinforcement) and rare items (peak emotional moments).
- **Themed Sets:** Collecting 5 related items to complete a "Set" provides a massive burst of satisfaction compared to collecting 5 random items.

## 5. Implementation Guidelines
- Implement a central "Museum" or "Compendium" feature early in the game.
- Ensure every major system (fishing, farming, exploring) feeds into the collection system.
- Provide lore or narrative flavor text for every collected item to increase its emotional value.

## 6. Player Journey
- **Early Game:** Player collects items by accident while doing other things.
- **Mid Game:** Player begins actively seeking out missing items to complete easy sets.
- **End Game:** Collection becomes a primary driver; player optimizes routes to find the rarest items.

## 7. Behavioral KPIs
- Percentage of players engaging with the Compendium UI.
- Average completion percentage of collections by D30.

## 8. Success Metrics
- High D90 retention driven by "completionist" players.
- Community collaboration (wikis, guides) to find hidden items.

## 9. Common Mistakes
- **The Impossible Item:** Making an item so rare (e.g., 0.001% drop rate) that completion feels impossible, causing players to give up entirely.
- **Inventory Clutter:** Forcing players to sacrifice precious inventory space to hold collection items. Collections should automatically track in a separate UI.

## 10. Ethical Considerations
We do not use collection mechanics to drive "gacha" monetization. Collections must be completable through gameplay, exploration, and effort, not through credit card swipes.

## 11. Future Expansion
Seasonal collections and community-wide collection goals.
