# Progression System

## 1. Vision
Progression is the heartbeat of a game. It must feel like genuine growth and mastery, not just watching numbers go up on a spreadsheet.

## 2. Behavioral Theory
Players need a sense of forward momentum. If the effort required increases, but the player's capability (or the perceived reward) does not increase proportionally, they experience burnout.

## 3. Psychological Foundation
- **Goal-Setting Theory:** Specific, challenging goals lead to higher performance and engagement than vague goals.
- **Competence (Self-Determination Theory):** The fundamental human need to feel effective and capable.

## 4. Design Principles
- **Milestone Pacing:** Progression should not be linear. It should have periods of rapid growth followed by plateaus that require mastery, culminating in major milestones.
- **Qualitative Upgrades:** Upgrades should change *how* the player plays, not just *how fast*. (e.g., A new tool that unlocks a new area, rather than a tool that just harvests 10% faster).
- **Visible History:** Players must be able to look back and see how far they've come (e.g., comparing their Day 1 shack to their Day 30 mansion).

## 5. Implementation Guidelines
- Structure progression into tiers. Moving within a tier is quantitative (numbers go up). Moving between tiers is qualitative (new mechanics unlocked).
- Use UI to clearly forecast the next major milestone so the player always has a short-term and mid-term goal.

## 6. Player Journey
- **Early Game:** Rapid progression, frequent level-ups.
- **Mid Game:** Progression slows; shifts from individual character level to base/town/meta level.
- **End Game:** Progression becomes horizontal (collecting variants, optimizing layouts) rather than vertical (higher levels).

## 7. Behavioral KPIs
- Time to reach major progression milestones (Tiers).
- Drop-off rates at specific progression plateaus.

## 8. Success Metrics
- Smooth retention curve without massive cliffs at progression gates.
- Players citing a feeling of "accomplishment."

## 9. Common Mistakes
- **The Grind Wall:** Suddenly multiplying the XP/resources required for the next level by 10x without introducing new mechanics to help achieve it.
- **Meaningless Levels:** Reaching level 50 feels exactly the same as level 5.

## 10. Ethical Considerations
We do not sell "skips" for progression walls that we intentionally made tedious. If a part of the game is boring enough that players will pay to skip it, we must redesign the game, not monetize the boredom.

## 11. Future Expansion
Dynamic progression scaling based on player engagement patterns to keep flow state optimal for both hardcore and casual players.
