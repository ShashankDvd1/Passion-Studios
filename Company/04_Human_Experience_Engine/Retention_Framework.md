# Retention Framework

## 1. Vision
Retention is the ultimate metric of a game's experiential quality. If players are leaving, the experience has failed. This framework defines the specific emotional milestones required to retain players at each stage of their lifecycle.

## 2. Behavioral Theory
A player's motivation for playing on Day 1 is entirely different from their motivation on Day 30. Game design must shift to match these evolving motivations.

## 3. Psychological Foundation
- **Novelty vs. Mastery:** Early retention is driven by the dopamine of novel stimuli. Late retention is driven by the deep satisfaction of mastery, identity, and social connection.

## 4. Design Principles & Retention Milestones
| Milestone | Player Motivation | Emotional Objective | Focus System |
|-----------|-------------------|---------------------|--------------|
| **D1 (Day 1)** | "Is this fun?" | Wonder, Curiosity | Core Experience Loops, Delight |
| **D3** | "Do I understand this?"| Competence, Comfort | Onboarding completion, clear UI |
| **D7** | "Does this fit my life?"| Routine, Anticipation | Habit Formation, early Meta Loops |
| **D14** | "What's the long game?"| Progression, Purpose | Progression System, unlocking new features |
| **D30** | "This is my world." | Identity, Ownership | Ownership System, Customization |
| **D90+** | "These are my people."| Belonging, Mastery | Collection System, Social features |

## 5. Implementation Guidelines
- Design specific "hooks" to pull players across the chasms between milestones.
- Example: A feature introduced on Day 2 that takes 5 days to resolve is a hook for D7 retention.

## 7. Behavioral KPIs
- D1, D3, D7, D14, D30, D90 Retention percentages.
- Churn hazard rate at specific progression levels.

## 8. Success Metrics
- Industry-leading D30 retention (>15% for casual/simulation genre).
- Flat long-term retention curve (minimal drop-off between D30 and D90).

## 9. Common Mistakes
- **Front-loading all content:** Showing everything on Day 1, leaving nothing to motivate D14 retention.
- **Ignoring the D7 shift:** Failing to pivot the game from "learning mechanics" to "expressing identity" around the one-week mark.

## 10. Ethical Considerations
Retention must be earned through value delivered to the player, not forced through sunk-cost fallacies or manipulating players who are trying to churn.

## 11. Future Expansion
Predictive churn modeling: Using machine learning to identify the behavioral patterns of players who are likely to churn in the next 3 days, and deploying targeted (non-monetized) Delight interventions.
