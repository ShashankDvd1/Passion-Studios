# Social Psychology

## 1. Vision
Single-player games create fans; social games create communities. We aim to foster a sense of belonging and positive interdependence without the toxicity of hyper-competitive PvP environments.

## 2. Behavioral Theory
Human beings are inherently social. We derive massive satisfaction from helping others, being recognized, and contributing to a group goal.

## 3. Psychological Foundation
- **Reciprocal Altruism:** People naturally want to return favors. If I water your crops, you feel a psychological drive to water mine.
- **Social Proof:** We look to others to determine what is valuable or correct.
- **In-Group Bias:** We form strong bonds with those we perceive to be on "our team."

## 4. Design Principles
- **Asynchronous Cooperation:** Players should be able to help each other even when not online at the same time (e.g., leaving a gift, speeding up a timer).
- **Positive-Only Interactions:** Systemically prevent griefing. Players should only have verbs to *help* each other, never to *harm* or steal.
- **Shared Goals:** Community milestones where all players contribute to a global unlock (e.g., "Donate 1M wood to build the bridge").

## 5. Implementation Guidelines
- The game must be entirely playable solo, but playing socially should provide efficiency bonuses and unique emotional rewards.
- Allow for deep self-expression (Identity) so that social visits are visually interesting.

## 6. Player Journey
- **Early Game:** Solo play, focusing on competence.
- **Mid Game:** Asynchronous interactions (visiting friends, trading).
- **End Game:** Deep community involvement, mentoring new players.

## 7. Behavioral KPIs
- Percentage of players engaged in guilds/clubs/friend lists.
- Number of asynchronous social interactions per DAU.

## 8. Success Metrics
- Strong out-of-game community (Discord, Reddit) focused on sharing and helping.
- Higher LTV (Lifetime Value) among socially active players compared to solo players.

## 9. Common Mistakes
- **Forced Socialization:** Making progression impossible without spamming Facebook friends.
- **Competitive Toxicity:** Introducing zero-sum mechanics (where one player's win is another's loss) into a cozy/relaxing game.

## 10. Ethical Considerations
We must protect vulnerable players (especially younger demographics) by restricting unstructured text chat and relying on curated emotes or positive-only mechanical interactions where possible.

## 11. Future Expansion
Implementation of "Neighborhoods" or small-scale persistent social spaces for 10-20 players.
