# Emotion Framework

## 1. Vision
Every feature must intentionally create a specific emotion. This framework defines the palette of positive emotions Wonder Forge Studios aims to evoke, ensuring our games are emotionally resonant and memorable.

## 2. Behavioral Theory
Emotions create memories, and memories create retention. Players do not remember the exact math of a reward drop, but they remember how they felt when it happened.

## 3. Psychological Foundation
Based on the Circumplex Model of Affect (Russell), we target specific quadrants of arousal and valence, heavily favoring high-valence (positive) emotions, balancing between high-arousal (Delight) and low-arousal (Comfort).

## 4. Design Principles
Every feature MUST be tagged with at least one Target Emotion from the following list:
- **Wonder:** Awe at the scale, beauty, or mystery of the world.
- **Curiosity:** The drive to explore the unknown.
- **Ownership:** Attachment to what the player has built or curated.
- **Discovery:** The joy of finding a hidden connection or item.
- **Comfort:** The safety of a familiar, relaxing routine.
- **Surprise:** Unexpected, positive breaks in pattern.
- **Pride:** Satisfaction in achievement or expression.
- **Progress:** The feeling of moving forward and growing.
- **Collection:** The satisfaction of completing a set.
- **Identity:** Seeing oneself reflected in the game world.
- **Mastery:** The realization of increased skill or knowledge.
- **Belonging:** Connection to the community or NPCs.
- **Creativity:** The joy of unrestricted expression.
- **Calm:** Relief from cognitive stress; tranquility.
- **Anticipation:** Looking forward to a future positive event.
- **Delight:** A sudden spark of joy from polish and micro-interactions.

## 5. Implementation Guidelines
- **Art & Audio:** Must directly support the Target Emotion. (e.g., "Calm" requires ambient audio, soft color palettes).
- **Anti-Emotions:** Features must be audited to ensure they do NOT evoke Anxiety, Guilt, Frustration, Boredom, or Regret.

## 6. Player Journey
- **Session Start:** Comfort, Anticipation.
- **Core Loop:** Progress, Curiosity.
- **Peak Moments:** Surprise, Delight, Wonder.
- **Session End:** Satisfaction, Calm.

## 7. Behavioral KPIs
- Qualitative sentiment tracking (surveys).
- Engagement spikes during specific features.

## 8. Success Metrics
- High correlation between intended emotion and player feedback in playtests.
- Increase in positive organic word-of-mouth (players describing the game using our target emotion words).

## 9. Common Mistakes
- **Emotional Flatness:** Creating a feature that works perfectly but evokes nothing.
- **Conflicting Emotions:** Designing a relaxing visual space (Calm) but adding a stressful ticking timer (Anxiety).

## 10. Ethical Considerations
We must never use dark emotions (fear of missing out, guilt for abandoning NPCs, anxiety over resource loss) as levers for engagement or monetization.

## 11. Future Expansion
Mapping specific color palettes, chord progressions, and UI animations to each of the 16 target emotions to create a unified sensory design language.
