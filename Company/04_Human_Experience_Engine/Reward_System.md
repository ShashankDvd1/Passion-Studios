# Reward System

## 1. Vision
Rewards in Wonder Forge games go beyond gold coins. We view rewards as a spectrum of functional, emotional, and social feedback designed to validate the player's time and effort.

## 2. Behavioral Theory
Over-reliance on extrinsic functional rewards (currency) destroys intrinsic motivation (the joy of play). A balanced reward system utilizes a taxonomy of diverse reward types to maintain long-term engagement.

## 3. Psychological Foundation
- **Overjustification Effect:** Giving a predictable extrinsic reward for an activity the player already enjoys reduces their intrinsic motivation to do it.
- **Variable Ratio Reinforcement:** Unpredictable reward schedules create the highest rate of engagement.

## 4. Design Principles (Reward Taxonomy)
Every reward must be classified into one of these types:
1. **Functional Rewards:** Currencies, tools, resources. (Use sparingly to avoid inflation).
2. **Emotional Rewards:** A character thanking the player, a beautiful animation, a piece of lore.
3. **Visual/Audio Rewards:** Confetti, satisfying sound effects.
4. **Social Rewards:** Badges, titles, leaderboard positions, ability to gift others.
5. **Discovery Rewards:** Finding a new area or a secret mechanic.
6. **Collection Rewards:** The final piece of a set.
7. **Narrative Rewards:** Advancing the story.
8. **Surprise Rewards:** Unexpected gifts for no reason.
9. **Long-Term Rewards:** Slow-burning investments paying off.
10. **Invisible Rewards:** Changes to the world state (e.g., the sky getting bluer as corruption is cleared).
11. **Identity Rewards:** Cosmetics, housing decorations.

## 5. Implementation Guidelines
- **The 3-to-1 Rule:** For every functional reward, there should be at least three non-functional (emotional, visual, discovery) rewards.
- **Diminishing Returns:** Functional rewards should decrease over a long session to encourage a healthy stopping point.

## 6. Player Journey
- **Early Game:** High density of functional and visual rewards to establish the core loop.
- **Mid Game:** Shift toward discovery, collection, and identity rewards.
- **End Game:** Driven entirely by social, narrative, and long-term invisible rewards.

## 7. Behavioral KPIs
- Reward claim rates.
- Resource inflation rate (tracking functional reward balance).

## 8. Success Metrics
- Players citing emotional or discovery moments as their favorite parts of the game, rather than just "getting rich."

## 9. Common Mistakes
- **The Treadmill:** Constantly increasing functional rewards (and costs) resulting in inflation, making early progress feel meaningless.
- **Extinguishing Intrinsic Joy:** Paying the player to do something they would have done for fun.

## 10. Ethical Considerations
We do not use variable ratio reinforcement (slot machine mechanics) for monetization. Variable rewards are strictly used for gameplay discovery (e.g., finding rare bugs in the garden), not purchasing power.

## 11. Future Expansion
Dynamic reward weighting based on player segment (e.g., offering more identity rewards to "Decorators" and more functional rewards to "Min-Maxers").
