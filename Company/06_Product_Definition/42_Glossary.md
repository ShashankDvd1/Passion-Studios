## 16. Implementation Notes (Definitions)
- **Headless Architecture:** Code logic that is completely decoupled from Unity `MonoBehaviour` UI, Audio, or VFX. It relies entirely on generic C# events.
- **Design Token:** A predefined, reusable standard for interaction feedback (e.g., `TKN-002: Micro-Reward`) mapped to psychological emotions.
- **Human Curiosity Framework (HCF):** The studio philosophy that every session must end with an unanswered question to drive anticipation and retention.
- **Progressive Disclosure:** The UI/UX principle of hiding advanced mechanics until the player specifically engages with a prerequisite action.
- **Vibe Coding:** AI-assisted development where the Founder dictates intent and reviews logic, while AI generates the boilerplate and systems.
- **Studio Experience Guidelines (SEG):** The ultimate UX constitution (Accessibility by Default, Clarity First, One-Hand Friendly).
- **Game OS (Phase 5):** The shared Unity framework that will power multiple rapid-release hybrid-casual games (e.g., Conveyor Chaos) before finalizing Tiny World.
