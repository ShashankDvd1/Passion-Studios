# Company Vision

## 1. Vision

Wonder Forge Studios exists to build tiny living worlds that people carry in their pockets and hold in their hearts.

We are not a game studio in the traditional sense. We do not employ armies of programmers or license expensive middleware or compete on production budgets. We are an AI-native company built on the conviction that the future of interactive entertainment belongs to small teams with extraordinary leverage — teams that use artificial intelligence not as a novelty but as the foundational operating layer for every aspect of the business.

Our 10-year vision:

**Year 1–2.** Launch Tiny World as proof that a single founder, supported by an AI-native operating system, can ship a commercially viable mobile game that players genuinely love. Validate the Tiny World Engine, Behavior Engine, and Agent OS as reusable infrastructure.

**Year 3–5.** Ship 3–5 additional games on the same engine and behavioral framework, each requiring less than half the effort of the previous title. Reach a combined 10M+ monthly active players. Demonstrate that AI-native development achieves 80–90% code and systems reuse across titles.

**Year 5–10.** Operate a portfolio of 10+ living worlds. License or open-source the Tiny World Engine and Behavior Engine to other indie studios. Become the reference case for AI-native game development at scale.

The goal is not size. The goal is leverage. One team. Many worlds. Maximum player joy per human hour invested.

## 2. Problem Statement

The mobile game industry is dominated by two unsustainable models:

**Mega-studios** spend $10M–$50M per title, employ hundreds of specialists, and still face a 95%+ failure rate. Their overhead means they cannot experiment. Their investors demand proven formulas. Innovation stalls.

**Hyper-casual factories** ship games in weeks, chase CPIs, and treat players as disposable impressions. There is no craft. No emotional attachment. No long-term relationship. Revenue evaporates as soon as ad budgets pause.

Both models waste human potential — the first through bureaucracy, the second through cynicism.

The gap: There is no established model for a tiny team to ship emotionally resonant, long-lived mobile games with deep behavioral design — repeatedly, sustainably, and profitably. AI changes this.

## 3. Goals

1. **Prove the AI-native studio model.** Demonstrate that a founder + AI operating system can produce game quality comparable to a 15–25 person traditional team.
2. **Build reusable infrastructure first.** The Tiny World Engine, Behavior Engine, and Agent OS are the real products. Every game is a showcase.
3. **Create emotional attachment, not addiction.** Players should feel wonder, ownership, and belonging — not compulsion or guilt.
4. **Ship Tiny World within 12 months** of development start to validate the entire thesis with real player data.
5. **Design every system for reuse.** No one-off solutions. Every module, pattern, and pipeline must transfer to the next game with minimal modification.
6. **Build a company that scales without headcount.** Revenue per employee should exceed industry benchmarks by 3–5x through AI leverage.

## 4. Non-Goals

- **We are not building a AAA studio.** We will never compete on graphical fidelity or cinematic production. Our competitive arena is behavioral design, emotional resonance, and operational efficiency.
- **We are not building a hit-driven business.** We do not need a single massive hit. We need a portfolio of sustainable, emotionally engaging products.
- **We are not building an ad-tech company.** Monetization is a necessary component, not the product. We will never optimize for ad revenue at the expense of player experience.
- **We are not chasing trends.** We do not pivot to whatever genre is trending on the top charts. We build from our behavioral framework and let the genre serve the emotional design.
- **We are not building for investors first.** Capital efficiency and profitability matter more than growth-at-all-costs metrics designed to impress Series A investors.

## 5. Principles

1. **Worlds over games.** We build places players want to return to, not puzzles players need to solve. The world is the product; gameplay supports the world.
2. **Emotion over engagement.** Retention is a byproduct of emotional attachment, not a goal achieved through manipulation. If a player returns because they feel warmth, we have succeeded. If they return because we manufactured anxiety, we have failed.
3. **Leverage over labor.** Every hour of human effort should produce disproportionate output. AI handles volume. Humans handle taste.
4. **Reuse over reinvention.** Building something once and using it ten times is ten times better than building it ten times.
5. **Small over big.** Constraints force creativity. A small team with the right tools will always out-innovate a large team with the wrong process.
6. **Data over debate.** When opinions conflict, design an experiment. Let player behavior decide.
7. **Trust over extraction.** Long-term player trust generates more lifetime revenue than short-term extraction. Always.

## 6. Requirements

1. Every product must be built on the shared Tiny World Engine.
2. Every engagement system must be designed using the Behavior Engine framework.
3. All development workflows must operate through the Agent OS.
4. The founder retains final authority on product vision, monetization ethics, and publishing decisions as defined in `00-001: Founder Charter`.
5. All documentation must follow the company document standard (YAML frontmatter + 14 mandatory sections).
6. The studio name must remain configurable across all documentation and systems until branding is finalized.
7. All technical architecture must follow composition-over-inheritance, data-driven design, and event-driven communication patterns.

## 9. Risks

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|------------|
| AI-generated code quality insufficient for production | Medium | High | Rigorous review pipeline (00-004), quality standards (02-004), automated testing requirements |
| Single founder bottleneck on all decisions | High | High | Decision Framework (00-003) delegates P2/P3 decisions to agents. Founder focuses on P0/P1 only |
| Market timing — cozy/simulation genre saturation | Medium | Medium | Behavior Engine provides differentiation regardless of genre. Design from psychology, not genre conventions |
| Engine reuse thesis fails — games too different | Low | High | Module Registry (Phase 3) ensures loose coupling. Core modules designed for extension, not modification |
| Player acquisition costs exceed LTV | Medium | High | Organic-first strategy. Community building. ASO optimization. Low CPI creative testing |
| AI tooling evolves faster than our architecture | Medium | Medium | Agent OS designed for tool-agnostic orchestration. Swap models without rewriting agents |

## 10. Future Expansion

- **Multi-platform expansion.** Mobile-first, but the engine should not prevent future PC, console, or web deployment.
- **Licensing model.** By Year 3–5, evaluate licensing the Tiny World Engine + Behavior Engine to other indie studios as a secondary revenue stream.
- **Community-created worlds.** Long-term, explore player-created content using the engine's modular architecture.
- **AI-generated content at scale.** As generative AI improves, increase the percentage of game content (art, narrative, level design) that is AI-produced with human curation.
